# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Men in and around Thessaloniki experiencing hair loss or thinning (receding hairlines, crown thinning, the shaved-look density they want back). Two states dominate:

- **Reassurance-seekers**: already sold on scalp micropigmentation (SMP), now choosing a specialist they can trust locally. They need confidence that this person's work is real, safe, and discreet before they commit.
- **Considerers**: thinning and curious, weighing whether SMP is right for them and whether it will look natural rather than fake.

Their context is private and a little vulnerable: usually browsing alone, on mobile, comparing options. Hair loss is sensitive, so the experience must never feel like a sales floor. The site is bilingual (Greek and English); both audiences are equally real.

## Product Purpose

DS Scalp Micropigmentation is the marketing presence for a premium SMP specialist. Its single job is to convert a skeptical, private visitor into a booked consultation. Success is the visitor reaching out, primarily through the consultation/contact request, with WhatsApp as a low-friction secondary path. Every page (home, about, services, gallery, FAQ, contact) exists to build credibility and lower the perceived risk of an irreversible cosmetic procedure, then make the booking ask feel like the natural next step. The design IS the product: trust is communicated visually before a single word is read.

## Brand Personality

Editorial luxury, masculine, quietly confident. Three words: **refined, credible, discreet.** The voice is calm expert authority, never hype. Emotionally the interface should evoke reassurance and aspiration: "this is handled by someone serious, and the result will look like me, not like a treatment." High-end editorial craft (serif display, charcoal/slate foundation, a single restrained accent, generous space, film grain) signals premium without shouting. Warmth comes through respect and honesty, not friendliness gimmicks.

## Anti-references

- **Cheap clinic / hard-sell**: no neon "BOOK NOW!!!", no stock smiling models, no before/after spam wall, no discount-banner urgency. Pressure tactics destroy trust here.
- **Generic SaaS / AI-template look**: no default Tailwind/shadcn uniform card grids, no gradient hero blobs, no hero-metric counters, nothing that reads as auto-generated.
- **Trendy, loud, or gimmicky**: no brutalism, neon, or heavy showy animation. Anything that undercuts a premium, trustworthy feel is wrong.
- Also avoid the cold, sterile hospital extreme (pure white + clinical teal, intimidating) even though clinical credibility matters; warmth and restraint coexist.

## Design Principles

1. **Show, don't tell.** Real results and genuine outcomes carry the argument. The gallery and authentic detail convince more than marketing claims ever will.
2. **Expert confidence, no hype.** Calm authority over exclamation marks. Trust is earned through restraint, craft, and precision, never through pressure.
3. **Discretion first.** Hair loss is sensitive. The experience stays respectful, private, and judgment-free at every step, including the booking ask.
4. **Earn the booking, then ask.** Build credibility before the conversion request; the CTA should feel like a natural next step, not an interruption.
5. **Bilingual parity (EL/EN).** Greek and English experiences must both feel native and first-class. Layout, typography, and tone hold up equally in either language; neither is an afterthought.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA: sufficient color contrast (mind the slate-blue accent on light backgrounds), full keyboard navigation, meaningful alt text on result imagery, and proper semantic structure. Motion is part of the brand feel (fade-up reveals, scroll-triggered transitions) and is a priority, but it must degrade gracefully: honor `prefers-reduced-motion` so reduced-motion users get a calm, fully usable, content-complete experience with no loss of information.
